Gjokica Stojanovski-Autobiography

My name is Gjokica Stojanovski.I was raised in a small rural town in New Hampshire that is part of what’s known as the Upper Valley, a cluster of small towns and villages in both New Hampshire and Vermont that are divided by the Connecticut River. The city of Lebanon, like many small New England towns included in the “UV”, contains a rich history deriving from its early colonial settlement and the presence of Dartmouth College attend Antioch College in Yellow Springs, Ohio.

My relationship with the guitar began in high school at the suggestion of my high school guidance counselor. I was lacking enough credits to graduate, and was feeling disillusioned with high school, my classes and American culture in general.

Since 2012, I have continued to study with El Entri and other Flamenco artists such as Esau Saltos and Victor Puebla Velazquez, performing concerts in Madrid Spain and New England, and I continue to compose my own music.
